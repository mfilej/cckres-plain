One  of  the  consequences  of  industrialisation  in  Slovenia  was  a  new  class  of  people  –  wage  labourers .  In  the  early  19th  century ,  the  discovery  of  coal  in  Zasavje  triggered  rapid  population  growth  that  could  not  be  integrated  into  the  rural  environment .  In  the  first  half  of  the  19th  century ,  the  owners  of  coalmines  tried  to  solve  housing  problems  by  building  individual  workers ’  houses ,  small  wooden  shacks ,  or  they  re - arranged  derelict  industrial  objects  into  apartments .  In  the  second  half  of  the  19th  century ,  housing  policy  was  implemented  in  accordance  with  the  development  plans  of  the  mine  and  factories .  Systematic  building  of  workers ’  neighbourhoods  ( colonies )  increased ,  especially  after  1873 ,  when  the  Trbovlje  coalmine  company  Trifailer  Kohlenwerks  Gesellschaft  ( Trbovlje  Coalmine  Society ,  TCS )  gradually  took  over  all  Zasavje  coalmines  and  established  a  monopoly  in  Slovenia .  The  basic  motifs  for  the  building  included :  the  provision  of  the  minimum  housing  quality ,  the  possibility  of  a  12hour  working  day  ( with  the  colonies  situated  in  the  immediate  vicinity  of  the  entrances  to  entrance  holes ) ,  the  recruitment  of  new  workers ,  the  prevention  of  economic  migration ,  lower  wages ,  and  the  prevention  of  wage  movements  and  strikes  that  could  follow  the  loss  of  housing .  Most  early  colonies  had  joint  kitchens ,  separated  from  the  rooms  and  located  in  a  common  hallway ,  and  common  toilets  for  several  families .  From  the  1890s  onwards ,  family  apartments  in  newly  built  buildings  within  the  colonies  comprised  a  room  and  a  kitchen ,  with  an  occasional  pantry  and  commonly  used  baker ’ s  ovens ,  waterwells  and  laundry  facilities .  This  was  the  living  standard  of  miners ’  families ,  prevailing  until  the  Second  World  War .  The  systematic  building  of  settlements  with  infrastructure  and  their  hygienic  control  raised  the  level  of  living  conditions  above  their  rural  surroundings .  However ,  the  houses  were  owned  by  employers ,  and  the  tenants  lost  their  housing  rights  with  the  termination  of  employment  ( discharge ,  change  of  employment ,  accident ,  death ) .  The  use  of  employer - built  houses  in  which  they  did  not  have  a  minimum  owner ’ s  share  bound  the  workers  to  their  company .  When  workers  became  superfluous  because  the  company  was  no  longer  profitable ,  the  burden  of  housing  was  transferred  to  the  state  or  to  the  municipality ,  which  had  to  take  care  of  dislodged  miners .
